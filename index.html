Why website usability matters

Websites are often created with good intentions, but over time updates, content changes and design decisions can introduce accessibility and usability issues.

For public sector organisations, accessibility is particularly important. Publicly funded websites in the UK are expected to meet WCAG accessibility standards so that services and information can be used by everyone.

For businesses, usability directly affects how visitors interact with a website. When people struggle to read content, navigate pages or complete tasks, they are more likely to leave the site without doing what they came to do.

Improving accessibility and usability helps websites work better for all visitors and can reduce the likelihood of people leaving before finding what they need.

What a review looks at

The review focuses on how people actually experience a website. This includes how easily visitors can find information, how clearly content is presented, and whether key tasks can be completed without confusion.

Common issues include unclear navigation, inconsistent page structure, poor colour contrast, and elements that are difficult to use on mobile devices. These problems are often small on their own but can combine to make a website frustrating to use.

Looking at a website with fresh eyes can help identify these issues quickly. The aim is not to redesign the site, but to highlight practical improvements that make it easier for visitors to understand and use.

Many of the most effective improvements are simple changes to wording, structure, or layout that make information clearer and easier to navigate.
