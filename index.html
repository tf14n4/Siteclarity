Why website usability matters

Websites are often created with good intentions, but over time updates, content changes, and design decisions can introduce accessibility and usability issues.

For public sector organisations, accessibility is particularly important. Publicly funded websites in the UK are expected to meet WCAG accessibility standards so that services and information can be used by everyone.

For businesses, usability directly affects how visitors interact with a website. When people struggle to read content, navigate pages, or complete tasks, they are more likely to leave the site without completing what they came to do.

Improving accessibility and usability helps websites work better for all visitors and can reduce the likelihood of people leaving before finding what they need.
