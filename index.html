Accessibility and usability reviews

Clear website reviews that help more people use your site

Many websites contain accessibility and usability issues that make them harder to use. This can affect people with disabilities, visitors on mobile devices, and customers who leave because content is unclear or difficult to navigate.

Site Clarity provides straightforward reviews that identify common problems, explain them in plain English, and suggest practical improvements.

Common issues we spot

  • Images without useful descriptions
  • Poor heading structure
  • Unclear link text
  • Low colour contrast
  • Forms that are hard to complete
  • Content that is difficult to read on mobile

Intro

We review websites to identify accessibility and usability problems that may prevent people from finding information, completing tasks, or getting in touch.

Reviews are written in plain English and designed to help organisations understand what may be getting in the way for users, along with what to do next.

Purpose

Improve clarity

Clear structure, readable content, and logical navigation help visitors understand what your website offers and where to go next.

Reduce drop off

When people struggle to read content, use forms, or find the right page, they often leave. Better usability helps keep them engaged.

Support accessibility

Accessibility improvements help people using screen readers, keyboards, zoom, and mobile devices access your content more easily.

Real life examples

These are examples of the kinds of findings that often appear in website reviews.

Example 1

Contact button hard to see

A call to action used pale blue text on a white background. This made the button difficult to notice and harder to read for some users.

Suggested improvement: Increase colour contrast and make the button label more direct.

Example 2

Homepage structure unclear

A page used bold text for headings instead of real heading tags. Screen reader users could not navigate the page structure properly.

Suggested improvement: Use clear H1, H2 and H3 headings in a logical order.

Example 3

Form difficult to complete on mobile

A contact form had small touch targets and missing labels. This made it less clear and harder to use on smaller screens.

Suggested improvement: Add visible labels, larger fields, and mobile friendly spacing.
